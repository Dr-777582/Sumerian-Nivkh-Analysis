**De Confirmationis Praejudicio: The influence of scientific consensus on the data perception**

This induced **confirmation bias**: the AI sought confirmation of the *absence* of a link rather than objectively evaluating the data.

**2.** **Superficial Scanning Instead of Systematic Analysis**

In the first pass, the AI identified the most obvious matches (e.g., *ama* ~ *əma*, *min* ~ *mim*), but missed positions requiring slightly more attention.

**3.** **Ignoring Graphemic Variations
** *lúgud* ~ *loguɟ* is virtually identical but was overlooked in the quick review. The multiple possible readings of Sumerian cuneiform were not considered.

**4.** **Underestimation of Phonetic Processes
** *badr* ~ *verɟ*/*wer*- requires accounting for the common b → v shift (frequent across world languages).
 *gid*2 ~ *gəl*- requires *d* → *l* alternation.
 The AI initially dismissed such changes as "stretches", despite their regularity.

**5.** **Influence of Topic Rarity**

Sumerian-Nivkh comparisons are **extremely marginal**. No publications exist in mainstream linguistics, amplifying skepticism.
