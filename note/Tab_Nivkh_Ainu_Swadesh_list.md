**Nivkh and Ainu Language Comparison (Based on Swadesh List)**

Researchers compared the first 100 positions of the Swadesh list between Nivkh and Ainu. Matches remain minimal, with many gaps in Ainu data. Forms differ substantially overall.

**Potential Weak Matches**

Researchers identified 5 instances of visual/phonetic similarity with partial overlap:

- I (1): *kuani*/*káni* (Ainu) ~ *ɲi* (Nivkh)     – weak (*k* ~ *ɲ*?)
- you (singular) (2): *eani* (Ainu) ~ *cʰi* (Nivkh)     – weak (*e* ~ *c*?)
- three (24): *re* (Ainu) ~ *caqř* (Nivkh)     – weak (*re* ~ *ca*?)
- big (27): *poro* (Ainu) ~ *pil-*, *pila*, *pilkar* (Nivkh)     – weak (*por* ~ *pil*)
- dog (47): *seta* (Ainu) ~ *qan* (Nivkh)     – weak (*s* ~ *q*?)

**Statistical Results**

A binomial test (*p**0* = 0.05, *n*=100, *k*=5) yields *p* ≈ 0.32—not statistically significant.

This contrasts sharply with Sumerian-Nivkh findings (41.9% matches, *p* < 0.01), underscoring isolates' variability.
