**Automated** **Comparison**

As an experiment, we filled the entire 100-word Swadesh list with lexical data from available sources as completely as possible. Many gaps remain due to the corpus-based nature of Sumerian (no native speakers, limited texts).

We compared Sumerian and Nivkh (including Proto-Nivkh) using Anthropic's Claude 3 AI tools. We instructed the AI to apply the algorithm mechanically to every Swadesh list position, ignoring prior beliefs.

**Evaluation** **Methodology**

Scoring System (0–5):

5.0: Nearly identical forms (Damerau-Levenshtein distance ≤ 1)

4.0–4.5: Very close with regular alternations

3.0–3.5: Close with typical changes

2.0–2.5: Possible connection, needs verification

1.0–1.5: Weak connection, doubtful

0–0.5: No similarity or data

Phonetic Processes Considered:

Lenition: *p* → *b* → *v*, *t* → *d*, *k* → *g*

Palatalization: *k* → *č*, *g* → *ž*

Rhotacism/lambdacism: *r* ↔ *l*, *n* ↔ *r*

Nasalization: vowel + *ŋ*

Prothesis and apocope

Consonant assimilation

Statistical Results:
 Of 86 comparable positions, we found 12 strong and 24 medium matches (total 36 = 41.9%). Random matches expect ~5–8%. The result proves statistically significant (*p* < 0.01).
